What do our classes consist of?

Karate training is generally organized into three categories. These categories, kihon, kata, and kumite, are always a part of our practices in some form.

  • Kihon (基本) represents the most foundational aspects of Karate and is vital to understand and master before continuing on. It consists of basic movements and forms which are the building blocks for everything that comes along later.

  • Kata (型) literally meaning "form" are sequences of movements that incorporate kihon. There are katas corresponding to each level and they progress in difficulty and length as the student progresses.

  • Kumite (組手) pulls together elements from Kihon and Kata to be used in sparring with an opponent. Kumite, just as with the others, increases in difficulty as the practicioner progresses.
